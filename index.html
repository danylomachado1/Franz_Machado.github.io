Research Experience

MAD(ish) Test Site Transparency Study

Advisor: Dr. Bryce Littlejohn (July 2023 - December 2023)
Department of Physics, Illinois Institute of Technology

This study aims to determine experiment parameters for using neutrinos to set limits on fission-derived yield in large explosions. By analyzing factors like distance, yield, and detector size, and using the PROSPECT-CNP toolkit, we predict flux and identify optimal combinations for different scenarios.

Data Transfer between LHCb Data Acquisition System and FPGA Accelerators

Advisor: Dr. Riccardo Fantechi (June 2021 - September 2021)
LHCb Computational Group, CERN, Switzerland

Reviewed the data acquisition system used by the LHCb experiment, performing tests on data transfer between PCIe40, EB, and the RETINA buffer. Developed efficient methods for merging data streams, leveraging characteristics of previous firmware runs.

Water Cherenkov Detector Calibration by Detected Muon Signals

Advisor: Dr. Cesar Castromonte, Luis Otiniano (October 2020 - March 2021)
Department of Science, Universidad Nacional de Ingeniería, Peru

Analyzed experimental data for vertical muons collected in Buenos Aires. Proposed a simulation for PMT response based on these analyses and simulations using CORSIKA and GEANT4.

A View through the PICO 40L Cameras

Advisor: Dr. Carsten Krauss, Scott Fallows (January 2020 - March 2020)
Department of Science, University of Alberta, Canada

Studied camera behavior in the detector to improve trigger efficiency, monitor stability, and identify phenomena for future analysis.
